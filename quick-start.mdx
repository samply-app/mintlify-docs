This guide walks you through the complete flow of getting audio in front of your listeners — from creating a fresh project to choosing the right sharing method for your situation. The whole process takes less than five minutes.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Create a project">
    Click the **New project** button in the sidebar to create a project, or press the `N` keyboard shortcut. Type a name for your project and press `Enter` to confirm.

    Each project acts as a single shareable container — think of it as one delivery, one album, one set of stems, or any logical grouping of audio you want to share together.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add tracks">
    Drag and drop your audio files directly onto the project. You can also drag in artwork at the same time. Files begin uploading immediately and are ready for playback as soon as the upload completes.

    <Tip>
      Drag tracks into the order you want before sharing — your listeners will see them in exactly that sequence. You can reorder by dragging at any time after upload as well.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share your project">
    Click the **Share** button in the top-right corner of the project page. The share dialog gives you two options for getting your project to listeners.

    ### Invite Collaborators (most secure)

    Invite someone directly by entering their email address, or generate a personal invite link to send them. Because access is tied to the individual, only the intended person can open the project. When inviting, assign one of three roles:

    - **Listener** — can play and view tracks only
    - **Editor** — can upload, rename, and reorder tracks
    - **Admin** — has full control, including sharing and settings

    ### Public Link

    Generate a public link that anyone can open without a Samply account. This is the fastest option for getting audio to someone quickly.

    <Warning>
      Public links can be forwarded — anyone who receives the link can access your project. To limit this risk, you can disable the link at any time or add a password that listeners must enter before they can play anything.
    </Warning>
  </Step>

</Steps>
